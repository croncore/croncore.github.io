The Brief from the Prime

The Prime Consultant had won a competitive RFP to develop a countywide Materials Management Plan, replacing the county's existing Solid Waste Plan under a recently amended state environmental statute. The new framework required a shift from disposal-centric planning to circular economy principles — recycling, food waste diversion, equitable access, and disaster debris readiness — with strict format and reporting rules set by the state environmental agency.

The Prime needed a remote partner who could move quickly on the data-intensive and document-heavy parts of the program without expanding its on-site team. Specifically, the Prime sought a team that could:

  • Synthesize a large body of legacy planning documents, agency datasets, and facility records into a clean analytical foundation.
  • Model the future waste stream — recycling, composting, and materials volumes — with defensible methodology.
  • Produce the final plan in the state agency's required format, including data visualizations, maps, and infographics in editable formats.
  • Operate confidentially under NDA, with all client-facing communication routed through the Prime's Project Manager.

What Croncore Did

Croncore staffed a remote technical team that integrated into the Prime's program structure, attending internal working sessions, reporting to the Prime's Project Manager, and operating behind the scenes throughout the engagement. Our contribution covered four workstreams:

01
AI-Driven Data Synthesis
We ingested the county's prior planning documents, sustainability records, and state agency datasets into a unified working environment. Generative AI accelerated the extraction, categorization, and cross-referencing of historical content into the structured summaries the Prime's team needed for downstream analysis. What would normally have taken weeks of manual review was compressed into days, with every output reviewed by a human analyst before handoff.
02
Facility Inventory & GIS Data Work
Croncore built and populated the facility inventory database to the field-level schema required by the state agency, and produced GIS layers in ArcGIS-compatible formats. The dataset became the analytical backbone for the gap analysis and forecast model, and was delivered to the Prime as a living asset the county could continue to use after plan adoption.
03
Forecast Modeling
We developed machine-learning forecast models projecting future waste stream, recycling, and composting volumes under multiple scenarios. The models supported the Prime's KPI design and were documented to a standard that allowed the Prime's team to defend the methodology to the planning committee and state agency reviewers.
04
Document Production & Visualizations
Croncore produced the data visualizations, maps, charts, and infographics required for the final plan, public-facing webpage content, and Prime-led presentations — delivered in PDF, InDesign, ArcGIS, Illustrator, Photoshop, Word, and Excel as required by the state agency's format rules. We supported the Prime's Project Manager through the public comment phase, processing input volume into structured summaries the Prime used to revise the draft plan.
